Operation

Multiple companies, projects and initiatives · 2001 - Present

Relentless improvement is the name of the game

  • I manically look for and implement better and better ways, solutions and procedures.
  • I am relentless at using time as efficiently as possible. I use techniques, tools and technology which help save a lot of time, or not waste time. I eliminate blockages, switch or drop tasks, when it makes more sense. Being stuck is a choice.
  • I constantly collect data, learn new things, improve my skills and deepen my knowledge.
  • I pay a lot of attention to cultural shifts and current trends.
  • I prefer fast and agile execution, as well as testing assumptions and products in real-life scenarios.

Real-life example


  • LinkedIn Poland Women wouldn't exist were it not for the initial idea, which was very, very different. Over several months our small team worked on my initial undeveloped concept, turning it into something we could call a project. We put a ton of work, time and heart into it, not knowing that a pivot was around the corner. We decided to give the new idea a shot, and the rest was history. Today LinkedIn Poland Women is among the fastest growing publications for women on LinkedIn, with a remarkable level of community engagement.

Paradigm Shift & Problem Solving

Multiple companies, projects and initiatives · 2001 - Present

"Why?", "Why not?", "Why not try?" are my favorite questions

  • I question all assumptions, which leads to better understanding, fewer mistakes and can save valuable resources.
  • I question the majority of other people’s and my own ideas, beliefs and thinking. This leads to unconventional and novel solutions.
  • I look for life hacks, often use certain tools and resources in a different, unexpected ways (adapt them to certain needs when necessary).
  • I will try certain things which at first glance seem unrealistic or hopeless, because it’s against our initial impulse to try them. The impulse tells me to give up or not test a certain idea, and I say "No way".
  • I never allow deals stop the parties from getting an even better outcome. You can always renegotiate a deal, and when you know how to make the situation better, you should do it.
  • I view laws (made or defined by humans) not as something which mirrors the actual reality. The laws don't tell you what the reality is or can be. It tells you what is expected.

Real-life example


  • I almost completely repurposed a project management app, after I had tested it for a couple of days. Working on projects, we used it almost entirely as our repository for ideas and resources, and everybody on our team loved it.

Management & Leadership

Multiple companies, projects and initiatives · 2001 - Present

I always pay attention to how resouces are used and money is spent

  • I manically protect all resources - talent, energy, money, time (best allocation, elimination of inefficiencies)
  • I manically monitor and cut costs, and come up with cheaper or free solutions and tools.
  • I always give my best on projects and causes I believe in, and I inspire others to believe and give their best also.

Real-life example


  • My remarkable work ethic, drive, almost infinite energy and positive attitude were central to the develompment of projects in which I was involved (especially in the early stages). LinkedIn Poland Women is the most recent example.

Team Building

Multiple companies, projects and initiatives · 2001 - Present

I build teams with talent, potential, efficiency and culture fit in mind

  • I look for people who: are driven · have the right kind of energy · show great potential · can both work and think (not merely wait for and follow instructions) · contribute something unique and necessary.
  • I part ways very quickly with the rest of people.

Real-life example


  • Working on projects, we had both - examples of a truly fantastic fit, and several close calls. We were able to proceed with speed and confidence thanks to our procedures and excellent communication.

Creativity, Design & Building

Multiple companies, projects and initiatives · 2001 - Present

My radar is always tuned to market opportunities, and then I act upon them

  • I initiate change by coming up with ideas and then developing and implementing appropriate solutions, procedures and tools.
  • I always look for low hanging fruits and emergent possibilities, and take advantage of them.
  • I treat creativity as the most important asset, and I always protect it. I know how it works - when it thrives, and when it dies.

Real-life example


  • Discord became our collaboration and communication tool after I decided to give it a shot. For our small team working on a small project it was great, and free! We learned how to use this tool, and soon after I started building servers there. A couple of weeks later the idea for a Discord community for women was born, which then became our LinkedIn-only publication. We kept Discord, but it became our main communication tool, plus we used it to record podcasts.

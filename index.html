I question everything

It's not something I force myself to do

I simply do it on a daily basis

01.

Do I question myself as well? Yes, however unlikely it may sound. This is why I continue to reinvent myself, and why at 48 I seek new challenges, instead of being in a steady job.

02.

It started with a steady profession though, one which people almost never leave, and which I did leave, after almost two decades. Law, a discipline for which I was well suited due to my natural ability to use logical reasoning in my arguments.

03.

Practicing law for so many years gave me a solid foundation, which I continue to leverage to this day, whenever certain circumstances call for it. For me even more important than knowing certain rules and how to interpret them was the realization that those rules inform us only about the expected reality. That's why we have all those mechanisms which we apply when law was broken. By the way, this is not a common realization.

Unplanned adventure

A life of seeking new opportunities

04.

Four qualities have always defined my approach to life: creativity, curiosity, entrepreneurship and strong bias for action (action over inaction). In other words:

  • I had to invent (ideas, projects, etc.)
  • I had to explore (collect data, analyze it, connect the dots)
  • I had to test (turn ideas into projects)
  • I had to solve problems (operate)
05.

Equipped with them probably from birth, I turned my professional life into an unplanned adventure by seeking new, interesting, challenging, all-consuming things to which I could devote my energy, time, and enthusiasm—and I could always find one. In addition, my enthusiasm almost always turned out to be contagious.

06.

My ability to confidently chart new territory without knowing the outcome, was the driving force behind my many creative endeavours. For example

  • in 2013 I launched Poland's first regular online publication on conflict resolution, and soon after, an entire project on conflict resolution for lawyers was born (discontinued),
  • also, in 2014 I co-created an event called Legal Market Day, which started as a concept to modernize the approach to educating lawyers,
  • in 2023 I co-created LinkedIn Poland Women, which currently is among the fastest growing publications for women on LinkedIn, with a remarkable level of community engagement.

There always has to be a first time

About this collaboration with AI

07.

I rarely have a clear idea of what to do next. Usually I need to initiate a change based on my intuition, and with no clear idea whatsoever. This means I need to take some initial action in order to generate momentum, which would then help me move forward. Soon, ideas emerge, followed by concrete goals.

08.

Once I had decided that I wanted to establish myself as an independent creative problem solver and change agent, I knew I had to stop my ongoing involvement in LinkedIn Poland Women. It will do fine, and there are other ways I can contribute to its development. So this was my initial step.

09.

I had very little experience creating professional resumes and hadn't done anything like it recently—I can't even recall the last time I did. Since I have a satisfying ongoing collaboration with AI and have been exploring and testing this technology pretty intensely recently, I decided AI would help me create everything from scratch. And we pulled it off.
